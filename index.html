Hej! I'm a PhD researcher, data scientist and statistician engaging with research spanning Bayesian learning theory, machine learning, epidemiology and ethical science. Alongside my contributions in these areas, I am interested in impactful ways that they can be applied to healthcare, social good and the arts.

Things I'm working on

COVID-19

Working with NHS daily site report data, we conducted a geotemporal survey of hospital bed saturation across England during the first wave of the COVID-19 Pandemic. This paper was featured in one of HDR's SAGE reports.

Investigating the potential impacts of different testing volumes, types and policies using extended epidemological models and expert knowledge in testing parameters and characteristics. There is an initial public-facing report and a dashboard to explore the models (coming soon). Additionally, funding has been secured from Microsoft for further work on pandemic preparedness and modelling in collaboration with MIT.

Data Science for Social Good

Assisting with the running of the Data Science for Social Good Fellowship in the UK through reviewing project partners spanning Ofsted, World Bank and more. Giving advice to current fellows and helping make evidential studies into impactful implementations.

Implementing Homeless Link research collaborating with QuantumBlack

Work carried out as a DSSG fellow in 2019. We augmented Homeless Link's platform for members of the public to refer rough sleepers to essential service providers (Pre-print of paper summarising the work and hopefully publication to follow). This work was presented at the DSSG DataFest at the shard in London and was to be presented at The Munich Data Festival before disruption due to COVID-19.


Fundamentals of Bayesian Inference

Collaborating with Chris Holmes and Jack Jewson to produce rigorous experimental and theoretical results in the applications of generalised Bayesian updating to learning from synthetic data.
